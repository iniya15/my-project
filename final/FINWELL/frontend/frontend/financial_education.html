Finance Education

Finance is the study and management of money, investments, and financial systems. It involves key activities such as:

Important Financial Terms

Retirement Planning

Preparing financially for life after work through saving, investing, and income strategies.

Pension

A steady retirement income, often provided by an employer or the government.

Social Security

Government-issued retirement benefits that provide essential monthly income.

Annuity

A financial product that provides regular income during retirement in exchange for a lump-sum or installment investment.

Withdrawal Rate

The annual percentage of savings a retiree withdraws, helping ensure money lasts throughout retirement.

Savings Rate

The portion of income regularly set aside for future needs, especially retirement.

Healthcare Costs

Medical and insurance expenses during retirement, often one of the biggest financial burdens.

Asset Allocation

The distribution of investments across categories (like stocks, bonds) to manage growth and risk.

Emergency Fund

A reserve of cash for unexpected expenses, offering financial security during crises.

Fixed Income

Stable and predictable income sources (e.g., pensions, bonds) vital for retirement budgeting.

Risk Tolerance

A person's comfort level with financial risk, guiding how conservative or aggressive their investment strategy should be.
